<p><p>Professional Billing refers to the billing process for medical services provided by individual healthcare professionals, such as doctors, therapists, or consultants. It typically involves billing for services rendered on an individual basis and is commonly used in private practice or small group practices.</p><p>Institutional Billing, on the other hand, involves the billing process for medical services provided by large healthcare organizations, such as hospitals, nursing homes, or clinics. It includes billing for a wide range of services, including inpatient/outpatient treatments, surgeries, medical equipment, and pharmaceuticals. Institutional billing often involves more complex and extensive coding and documentation requirements compared to professional billing.</p></p>
