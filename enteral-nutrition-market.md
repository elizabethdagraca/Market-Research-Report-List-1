<p><p>Enteral nutrition refers to the delivery of nutrients directly into the gastrointestinal tract through a feeding tube. It is typically used when a patient is unable to consume food orally or has a severely impaired ability to digest and absorb nutrients. This method of nutrition delivery provides a complete and balanced diet and can be administered through different types of tubes, such as nasogastric, nasojejunal, or gastrostomy tubes.</p><p>The enteral nutrition market has been experiencing steady growth in recent years and is expected to continue expanding at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 6.8% during the forecast period. Several factors contribute to the market's growth, including the increasing prevalence of chronic diseases, such as cancer, gastrointestinal disorders, and neurological disorders, which often require enteral nutrition. The aging population and the rise in the number of critically ill patients also drive the demand for enteral nutrition products.</p><p>Additionally, advancements in healthcare infrastructure and technologies, along with increasing awareness regarding the benefits of enteral nutrition, are further supporting market growth. The demand for specialized enteral nutrition formulas tailored for specific medical conditions is also rising. Manufacturers are focusing on developing innovative products to meet the specific nutritional needs of patients.</p><p>Moreover, the COVID-19 pandemic has significantly impacted the enteral nutrition market. With the high prevalence of the virus and the increased number of critically ill patients requiring nutritional support, the demand for enteral nutrition products has witnessed a surge. Healthcare facilities and home healthcare settings have been adopting enteral feeding methods to ensure adequate nutrition and minimize exposure risks.</p><p>In summary, the enteral nutrition market is projected to grow at a CAGR of 6.8% during the forecast period.
