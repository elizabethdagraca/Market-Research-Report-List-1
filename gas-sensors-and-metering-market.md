<p><strong>The Gas Sensors and Metering Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Electrochemical</li><li>Photoionization Detectors (PID)</li><li>Solid-state or Metal–Oxide–Semiconductor (MOS)</li><li>Catalytic</li><li>Infrared</li><li>Laser</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The gas sensors and metering market is categorized into several types based on the technology used. Electrochemical sensors measure gas concentration through chemical reactions. Photoionization detectors (PID) employ UV light to ionize gas molecules for detection. Solid-state or Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor (MOS) sensors use a thin film that changes resistance upon exposure to gases. Catalytic sensors detect combustible gases by measuring the heat generated during their oxidation. Infrared sensors detect gases by identifying specific wavelengths absorbed by the gas molecules. Laser sensors employ laser beams to detect gas concentrations. Other technologies include thermal conductivity, flame ionization, and acoustic sensors.</p></p>
