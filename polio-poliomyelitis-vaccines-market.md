<p><p>Polio vaccines are categorized into two main types: Inactivated Polio Vaccine (IPV) and Oral Polio Vaccine (OPV). IPV is composed of inactivated poliovirus and is given through injection. It protects against all types of poliovirus and is primarily used in countries with low risk of polio. OPV contains live, weakened poliovirus and is administered orally. It produces immunity in the intestine, preventing the virus from spreading to others. OPV is commonly used in countries with high polio transmission and for mass immunization campaigns.</p></p>
