<p><p>Pulmonary drug delivery devices/systems refer to medical devices or systems that are designed to deliver drugs directly to the lungs. These devices are used to administer various medications such as bronchodilators, corticosteroids, antibiotics, and vaccines, among others. They are primarily used for the treatment of respiratory diseases such as asthma, chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), cystic fibrosis, and respiratory infections.</p><p>The market for pulmonary drug delivery devices/systems is witnessing significant growth due to several factors. Firstly, the increasing prevalence of respiratory diseases globally is driving the demand for effective drug delivery systems. Additionally, the rising geriatric population, who are more susceptible to respiratory disorders, is further fueling market growth. Moreover, the growing demand for home healthcare services and the increasing preference for self-administration of medication are also contributing to market expansion.</p><p>In terms of trends, there is a rising focus on the development of innovative and advanced pulmonary drug delivery devices/systems. Manufacturers are investing in research and development to create devices that ensure precise dosing, enhanced drug deposition in the lungs, and improved patient compliance. For instance, dry powder inhalers (DPIs), metered dose inhalers (MDIs), and nebulizers with improved drug delivery mechanisms are being developed and launched in the market.</p><p>Furthermore, the COVID-19 pandemic has also impacted the pulmonary drug delivery devices/systems market. <p><p>Pulmonary drug delivery devices/systems market includes various types of devices used for delivering medication directly to the lungs. Metered dose inhalers (MDIs) are handheld devices that release a fixed dose of medication in aerosol form. Dry powder inhalers (DPIs) deliver medication in powder form, which is inhaled by the patient. Nebulizers are machines that convert liquid medication into a fine mist for inhalation. Accessories market pertains to additional components like masks, mouthpieces, and tubing used in conjunction with these devices.</p></p>
