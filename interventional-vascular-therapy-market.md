<p><p>Interventional vascular therapy refers to non-surgical procedures to diagnose and treat various vascular diseases, primarily those affecting the veins and arteries. It involves the use of minimally invasive techniques such as catheters, stents, and balloons to access and treat the affected blood vessels. This therapy is widely used to treat conditions such as peripheral artery disease, deep vein thrombosis, varicose veins, and arteriovenous malformations.</p><p>The global interventional vascular therapy market is projected to witness substantial growth during the forecast period. Several factors contribute to this growth, such as the increasing prevalence of vascular diseases, technological advancements in interventional procedures, the rise in the geriatric population, and the growing preference for minimally invasive treatments. Additionally, the adoption of endovascular techniques by healthcare professionals and the rising healthcare expenditure further propel market growth.</p><p>The market is also witnessing various trends that are expected to impact its growth positively. One such trend is the shift towards drug-eluting stents, which provide targeted drug delivery and reduce the chances of restenosis. Furthermore, the development of bioresorbable stents is gaining traction, as they dissolve over time and reduce the need for long-term medication. Other trends include the emergence of robotic-assisted interventions, the integration of imaging technologies for better visualization, and the focus on developing personalized treatment approaches.</p><p>In conclusion, the interventional vascular therapy market is anticipated to experience significant growth. Factors such as an increasing prevalence of vascular diseases and technological advancements are driving this growth.
