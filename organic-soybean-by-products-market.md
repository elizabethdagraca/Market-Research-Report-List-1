<p><p>Organic soybean by-products include soybean oil, oilcake, and lecithin. Soybean oil is a common vegetable oil extracted from soybeans, widely used for cooking and in various food products. Oilcake refers to the solid residue left after oil extraction from soybeans and is commonly used as animal feed due to its high protein content. Lecithin, derived from soybean oil, is a natural emulsifier commonly used in food processing to improve texture and stability. These organic soybean by-products play a significant role in various industries, offering diverse applications and nutritional benefits.</p></p>
