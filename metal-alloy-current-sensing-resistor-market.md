<p><p>Metal alloy current sensing resistors are widely used in various electronic devices and circuits to measure and control the flow of electric current. These resistors come in two main types based on their mounting styles - Through Hole and Surface Mount Device (SMD). Through Hole resistors are larger in size and are mounted by inserting their leads into holes on printed circuit boards. SMD resistors, on the other hand, are smaller and are mounted directly onto the surface of circuit boards. Both types offer reliable current monitoring and control solutions for different applications.</p></p>
