<p><p>Photostimulable Phosphor (PSP) Systems are imaging devices used in medical radiology to capture X-ray images. PSP systems are based on the principle of photostimulated luminescence, in which X-ray photons are absorbed by a photostimulable phosphor plate, causing the release of energy stored in the material. This released energy is then captured by a photomultiplier tube or a solid-state detector and converted into a digital image.</p><p>The Photostimulable Phosphor (PSP) Systems Market is expected to witness significant growth during the forecast period. One of the key factors driving market growth is the increasing demand for cost-effective medical imaging solutions. PSP systems offer advantages such as lower equipment and maintenance costs compared to other imaging modalities, making them a preferred choice for healthcare facilities, especially in emerging economies.</p><p>The growing prevalence of chronic diseases and the rising geriatric population also contribute to the market growth of PSP systems. As the incidence of diseases such as cardiovascular disorders and cancer increases, the demand for diagnostic imaging procedures rises, driving the need for PSP systems.</p><p>Furthermore, technological advancements in PSP systems, such as improved image quality, faster processing, and enhanced patient comfort, are also fueling market growth. The integration of PSP systems with Picture Archiving and Communication Systems (PACS) and electronic health records further enhances their efficiency and workflow.</p><p>In conclusion, the Photostimulable Phosphor (PSP) Systems Market is projected to experience significant growth during the forecast period.
