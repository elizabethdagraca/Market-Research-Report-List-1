<p><p>The enzyme-based plant protein market can be categorized into various types. Cellulase is an enzyme that breaks down cellulose, a component of plant cell walls. Lactase aids in the digestion of lactose, a sugar found in milk. Amylase breaks down starch into simple sugars. Pectinase breaks down pectin, a complex carbohydrate found in fruits and vegetables. Mannanases break down mannans, which are polysaccharides found in plant cell walls. Lastly, there are other enzymes that target different components of plant proteins to facilitate their digestion and utilization within the human body.</p></p>
