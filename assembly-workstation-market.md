<p><p>An assembly workstation is a designated area where products or parts are assembled. These workstations can be classified into two main types: manual and automatic. </p><p>In a manual assembly workstation, the assembly process is performed entirely by human operators. They manually handle the parts, apply tools, and use their expertise to complete the assembly. This type of workstation is typically used for smaller-scale production or when complex tasks require human skills.</p><p>On the other hand, an automatic assembly workstation utilizes machinery and automation technology to complete the assembly process. Robots and machines carry out tasks such as picking and placing parts, tightening screws, or performing repetitive actions. This type of workstation is commonly employed in high-volume production environments where efficiency and precision are crucial.</p></p>
