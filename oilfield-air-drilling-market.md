<p><strong>The Oilfield Air Drilling Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Dust Drilling</li><li>Foam Drilling</li><li>Mist Drilling</li><li>Aerated Drilling</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Oilfield Air Drilling is a technique used in drilling operations where air or gas is used instead of drilling fluids to remove cuttings from the wellbore. There are various types of air drilling methods employed in the market. Dust drilling involves using only air to remove cuttings, resulting in dry formation. Foam drilling uses air mixed with foam to create a stable foam-like substance that carries the cuttings out of the wellbore. Mist drilling involves pumping air mixed with mist, which helps to reduce friction and cool the drilling bit. Aerated drilling uses air mixed with a liquid, such as water or foam, to create a lightweight mixture that carries cuttings out of the wellbore.</p></p>
