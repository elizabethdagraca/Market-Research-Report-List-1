<p><p>The enterprise infrastructure market can be classified into three types: public, private, and hybrid markets. The public market refers to infrastructure that is owned and managed by third-party service providers, allowing multiple organizations to share resources. In contrast, the private market involves infrastructure owned and maintained internally by a single organization, providing complete control over data and resources. Lastly, the hybrid market combines elements of both public and private infrastructure, allowing organizations to leverage the benefits of both models while ensuring security and flexibility.</p></p>
