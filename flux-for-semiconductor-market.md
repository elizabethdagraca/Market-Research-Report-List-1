<p><p>Flux is a material used in the semiconductor industry to aid in the soldering process. Water-soluble and low-residue flux types are advantageous as they are easy to clean, leaving minimal residue on the surface. Rosin soluble flux contains rosin, a natural resin, which enhances solder joint quality and leaves a small amount of residual material. Epoxy flux, on the other hand, contains epoxy resin, providing excellent thermal and mechanical stability to the solder joint. All these flux types play a crucial role in ensuring efficient and reliable soldering in the semiconductor market.</p></p>
