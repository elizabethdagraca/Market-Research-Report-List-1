<p><p>Ultrasound biometer is a medical device that uses ultrasound waves to measure the dimensions of various parts of the eye, especially the anterior segment of the eye. It provides accurate measurements of the eye's axial length, corneal thickness, anterior chamber depth, and lens thickness. This information is vital in diagnosing and monitoring eye conditions such as glaucoma, cataracts, and myopia.</p><p>The global ultrasound biometer market is expected to witness significant growth during the forecast period. The increasing prevalence of eye diseases, growing geriatric population, and rising demand for accurate diagnostic tools are major factors driving market growth. Additionally, advancements in technology and healthcare infrastructure, as well as increasing awareness among patients, are contributing to the market's expansion.</p><p>Moreover, the market is witnessing several trends that further boost its growth. The integration of artificial intelligence (AI) technology with ultrasound biometers is improving their accuracy and precision, enhancing the diagnosis and treatment of eye conditions. Furthermore, the introduction of portable and handheld ultrasound biometers has made eye examination more accessible, particularly in remote areas or areas with limited healthcare infrastructure.</p><p>The market's growth is also driven by the increasing adoption of teleophthalmology, which allows healthcare professionals to remotely examine patients' eyes using ultrasound biometers.
