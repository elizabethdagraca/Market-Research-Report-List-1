<p><p>Bronchodilators are medications used to alleviate symptoms of respiratory conditions like asthma and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD). Short-acting bronchodilators provide quick relief by relaxing and opening the airways for a short duration, typically within a few minutes. Long-acting bronchodilators, on the other hand, provide extended relief over a longer period, usually up to 12-24 hours. These medications are commonly available in inhaler forms and work by expanding the bronchial tubes to promote easier breathing. Both types play a crucial role in managing respiratory disorders.</p></p>
