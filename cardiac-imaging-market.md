<p><p>Cardiac imaging refers to various non-invasive and invasive techniques used for diagnosing and evaluating heart conditions. Coronary catheterization involves threading a catheter through a blood vessel to assess blockages. Echocardiogram employs sound waves to create an image of the heart. Intravascular ultrasound visualizes blood vessels using a catheter with an ultrasound probe. Positron emission tomography indicates areas of heart muscle damage. Computed tomography produces detailed cross-sectional images of the heart. Magnetic resonance angiography employs magnetic fields and radio waves to generate images of blood vessels.</p></p>
