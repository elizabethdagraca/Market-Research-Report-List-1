<p><p>Connected fire detection systems refer to a network of sensors and equipment that are designed to detect and alert individuals of a fire outbreak in a building. There are two main types of connected fire detection systems: addressable and conventional. Addressable connected systems allow each device to have its own unique address, providing detailed information about the exact location of a fire. On the other hand, conventional connected systems use zones to indicate the general area of a fire. Both systems play a crucial role in ensuring timely detection and response to fire emergencies.</p></p>
