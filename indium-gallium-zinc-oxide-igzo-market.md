<p><p>Indium Gallium Zinc Oxide (IGZO) is a semiconductor material used in various electronic devices. Crystalline IGZO has a well-ordered atomic structure, allowing for higher electron mobility and better performance in applications like high-speed circuits. On the other hand, amorphous IGZO lacks a defined atomic structure, making it more suitable for flexible displays and touchscreens. Both types cater to different market needs, with crystalline IGZO offering superior performance and amorphous IGZO providing flexibility and versatility in electronic applications.</p></p>
