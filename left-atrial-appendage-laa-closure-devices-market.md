<p><p>Left atrial appendage (LAA) closure devices are medical devices used to close the LAA, a small, ear-shaped sac in the left atrium of the heart. <p><p>Left Atrial Appendage (LAA) Closure Devices are used to prevent blood clots from forming in the left atrial appendage of the heart, reducing the risk of stroke in patients with atrial fibrillation. There are two types of devices used for LAA closure: epicardial and endocardial. Epicardial LAA closure devices are implanted on the outside of the heart, while endocardial devices are placed inside the left atrial appendage. Both types aim to occlude or seal off the LAA, preventing the formation of blood clots and reducing the risk of stroke.</p></p>
