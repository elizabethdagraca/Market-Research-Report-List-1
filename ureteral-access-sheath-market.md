<p><p>A ureteral access sheath is a medical device used during urologic procedures to facilitate the delivery of other instruments, such as ureteroscopes or stone retrieval devices, to the urinary tract. It is a thin, flexible tube that is inserted through the urethra into the bladder and then navigated into the ureter. The sheath provides a working channel for the insertion and removal of other devices, minimizing trauma to the ureter and allowing for easier instrument manipulation.</p><p>The ureteral access sheath market is expected to witness steady growth in the coming years. The increasing prevalence of urologic diseases such as kidney stones and urinary tract infections is driving the demand for better diagnostic and treatment options. Ureteral access sheaths offer benefits such as reduced procedure time, improved visualization, and decreased risk of ureteral injury, thereby increasing their adoption in various urologic procedures.</p><p>Moreover, advancements in technology have led to the development of innovative ureteral access sheaths with features such as hydrophilic coatings, atraumatic tips, and larger working channels, enhancing their performance and patient comfort. These factors are further propelling market growth.</p><p>An increasing geriatric population, who are more prone to urologic diseases, is also contributing to the expansion of the ureteral access sheath market. Additionally, the rising number of minimally invasive surgeries and the growing preference for outpatient procedures are boosting the demand for ureteral access sheaths.</p><p>Overall, the ureteral access sheath market is expected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 4.1% during the forecast period, driven by the increasing prevalence of urologic diseases, technological advancements, and changing surgical practices.</p></p>
