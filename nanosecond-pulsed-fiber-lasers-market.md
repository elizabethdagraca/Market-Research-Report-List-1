<p><p>Nanosecond Pulsed Fiber Lasers are advanced laser systems that emit high-power laser pulses in the nanosecond range. The market types include Q-Switched Fiber Lasers and Mode-Locked Fiber Lasers. Q-Switched Fiber Lasers produce short but high-energy pulses by using a technique called Q-switching. These lasers find applications in materials processing, micromachining, and scientific research. On the other hand, Mode-Locked Fiber Lasers generate ultrashort pulses by locking the laser's light waves together. These lasers are utilized in fields such as telecommunications, nonlinear optics, and ultrafast spectroscopy.</p></p>
