<p><p>Temperature control valves are used in various industries to regulate the flow and temperature of fluids. Thermostatic valves are operated by temperature-sensitive elements that expand or contract, controlling the flow. Self-actuating valves use mechanical force from the fluid itself to open or close. Internally sensed valves have a built-in temperature sensor that detects the fluid temperature and adjusts accordingly. Electrically or pneumatically actuated valves are controlled by an external electrical or pneumatic signal. Each type has unique features and advantages, making them suitable for different applications in the market.</p></p>
