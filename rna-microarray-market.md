<p><strong>The RNA Microarray Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Oligonucleotide RNA Microarrays (oRNA)</li><li>Complementary RNA Microarrays (cRNA)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>RNA microarrays are widely used tools in gene expression studies. They can be categorized into two main types - Oligonucleotide RNA Microarrays (oRNA) and Complementary RNA Microarrays (cRNA). </p><p>oRNA microarrays utilize oligonucleotide probes, typically short nucleotide sequences, to capture and detect specific RNA molecules in a sample. These probes are designed to target specific genes or regions of interest.</p><p>On the other hand, cRNA microarrays use complementary RNA probes synthesized from the sample RNA molecules. The sample RNA is first reverse transcribed into cDNA and subsequently amplified to produce labeled cRNA probes, which are then applied to the microarray for gene expression analysis.</p><p>Both types of microarrays offer unique advantages and are suitable for different research objectives and applications in studying gene expression profiles.</p></p>
