<p><p>An alternating-current (AC) transformer is a device used to transfer electrical energy between two or more circuits through electromagnetic induction. It consists of two or more coils of insulated wire wound around a core made of magnetic material such as iron. The primary coil is connected to the input voltage, while the secondary coil delivers the output voltage.</p><p>The AC transformer market has been experiencing steady growth in recent years and is expected to continue expanding at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 12% during the forecast period. There are several factors contributing to this growth.</p><p>Firstly, the increasing demand for electricity, driven by rapid urbanization and industrialization, is fueling the need for efficient electricity transmission and distribution systems. AC transformers play a crucial role in stepping up or stepping down voltage levels according to the requirements of various applications, thereby enabling effective power flow.</p><p>Additionally, the rise of renewable energy sources such as wind and solar power is driving the adoption of AC transformers. These transformers are essential for converting the fluctuating AC output from renewable energy systems into a stable form suitable for grid integration.</p><p>Moreover, the advancements in the construction and design of AC transformers, aimed at enhancing operational efficiency and reducing energy losses, are further propelling the market growth. Manufacturers are focusing on developing compact and lightweight transformers with higher efficiency ratings to optimize energy consumption.</p><p>Furthermore, the increasing investments in infrastructure development and the need for grid modernization in emerging economies are creating opportunities for the AC transformer market. <p><p>The Alternating-current (AC) transformer market can be categorized into three types based on voltage levels: Ultra High Voltage (UHV), Extra High Voltage (EHV), and High Voltage (HV). UHV transformers are designed to handle extremely high voltages, typically above 800 kV. EHV transformers operate at voltages ranging from 245 kV to 765 kV. HV transformers handle high voltage levels ranging from 69 kV to 230 kV. each type serves specific power transmission needs and is crucial in safely and efficiently transmitting electricity across long distances.</p></p>
